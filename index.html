Program

Preliminary dates: June 30 & July 1

The Network Neuroscience satellite will take place between June 28 and July 2, with the most likely dates June 30/July 1. It will be held over 2 days to accommodate for different time zones for the 8-hour conference schedule: the first during US PM time, and the second during the US morning time. The poster session will happen at the end of the conference to allow participants to remain engaged in discussions as long as they wish (midday EST, morning west coast, afternoon europe, late night pacific).

Posters & Oral presentations


The 2021 Network Neuroscience Satellite will include a number of poster and oral presentations contributed by submission. For the poster sessions we are working to provide a friendly, robust, and inclusive platform to maximize the quality of the interactions. If you would like to present your work, please, follow this link to Easychair, select the type of contribution and submit your abstract by April 9th. Detailed information can be found below.

Submit your abstract

Abstracts should be in pdf format and no longer than 1 page (about 500 words). Please note that we have only a limited number of slots available for oral presentations, and encourage you to consider submitting your work as a poster. We will be holding a dedicated session for the posters.
Oral presentations will be selected by the program committee and added to our website as Contributed Talks. Presentations are planned for 15 minutes each.
If you would like to present your work, please, follow this link to Easychair, select the type of contribution and submit your abstract by April 9th.

We welcome submissions from all areas of Network Neuroscience, including (but not limited to):

  • Brain transcriptional and gene regulation networks;
  • Structural brain networks;
  • Functional brain networks;
  • Network theory, modeling and analysis;
  • Network communication and information flow;
  • Circuit dynamics;
  • Brain-behavior interactions;
  • Systems neuroscience;
  • Clinical applications.
